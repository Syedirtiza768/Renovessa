# Advertising Claim Substantiation

No objective claim may be published unless it has a unique claim ID, the exact or bounded claim language, intended audience/channel, responsible owner, evidence collected **before publication**, reasoning connecting that evidence to the claim, limitations/disclosures, approval date, and next review date.

The code attaches a claim ID and estimator model version to every generated range. `ESTIMATE_RANGE_REGISTER.md` is the controlling inventory. Its current entries are internal baselines marked pending source review; that status is not independent market proof. Before a range is approved for a paid advertisement or an unqualified accuracy claim, the owner must attach representative DMV invoices, accepted bids, supplier/labor data, or a documented statistically sound analysis and change the record to `APPROVED`.

## Change control

1. Marketing/product proposes exact claim language and claim ID.
2. Claim owner adds dated, unaltered source material under a restricted evidence location (personal data redacted) and summarizes methodology in the register.
3. A reviewer checks relevance, geographic/time coverage, sample bias, computation, disclosures, and consistency between evidence and every placement.
4. Code/content review verifies the ID exists and has not expired.
5. Material price/model/content changes create a new model version; never overwrite the historical record used for an earlier publication.
6. Withdraw or qualify expired, contradicted, or unsupported claims immediately and record the action.

Do not use testimonials, scraped ratings, competitor copy, AI output, or a disclaimer as a substitute for a reasonable evidentiary basis.
